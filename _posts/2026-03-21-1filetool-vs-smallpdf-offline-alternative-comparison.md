## Architecture: Cloud vs. Local

The fundamental difference between these two tools is where your files are processed.

**Smallpdf** is a web application. When you use it, your files are uploaded to Smallpdf's servers in the cloud, processed remotely, and then made available for download. This means you need an active internet connection, and your files travel through third-party infrastructure.

**1FileTool** is a native desktop application for macOS, Windows, and Linux. All processing happens on your local machine. Files never leave your computer, and the tool works perfectly with your internet disconnected.

This architectural difference influences every other aspect of the comparison.

## Privacy and Data Handling

### Smallpdf

Smallpdf encrypts files during transfer and states that files are deleted from their servers after a processing window. However, their privacy policy allows file retention for up to 14 days. During this period, your documents exist on servers in a data center you cannot inspect.

Smallpdf also collects analytics data including usage patterns, file types processed, and standard web tracking information. If you use the free tier, you may see ads, which involves additional third-party tracking.

### 1FileTool

1FileTool collects no data. There are no servers to retain files on because there are no servers. Files exist on your local disk before and after processing. There is no analytics, no tracking, no telemetry. The application does not even require an internet connection after installation (except for a one-time license activation for Pro users).

For anyone handling confidential documents, client data, or files covered by regulations like GDPR or HIPAA, the difference here is significant. Local processing eliminates the compliance question entirely.

## Speed

Cloud tools are fundamentally limited by network speed. Even on a fast connection, the upload-process-download cycle adds latency to every operation.

Local tools process at hardware speed. A modern laptop can merge, compress, or convert files in seconds without any network overhead. The difference is most noticeable with larger files (videos, high-resolution images) and batch operations.

For a single small PDF merge, the difference might be negligible. For compressing 50 images or converting a batch of video files, local processing is dramatically faster.
